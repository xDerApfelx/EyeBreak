# v0.1.0 — First Light 👁️

The first release of EyeBreak — a tiny tray app that makes sure you actually take the eye breaks your eye doctor keeps telling you about.

## ✨ What's New

### ⏱️ Automatic Break Timer
- Counts 60 minutes of *active* screen time (configurable), then calls for a 5-minute eye break
- Starts with your PC, lives in the tray — no manual timers to dismiss and forget
- Idle detection: step away and the timer pauses; stay away long enough to count as a break and the hour restarts

### 👻 Overlay
- Appears in the last 10 minutes (or permanently, if you prefer) — even over borderless-fullscreen games
- Click-through by default: it never steals your mouse. Hover to reveal the buttons
- Escalating visuals: gentle pulse at 5 minutes, urgent pulse at 1:30

### 🎚️ Difficulty Levels
- 🟢 **Easy** — dismiss instantly, postpone forever
- 🟡 **Normal** — postpone twice, skip unlocks after 30 seconds
- 🔴 **Strict** — no way out: when time is up, your PC gets locked (nothing is closed, just log back in)

### 🎮 Game & Flow Awareness
- Detects installed games from Steam, Epic, GOG, Ubisoft Connect, Xbox and Battle.net — in a game you get one extra postpone to finish the round
- Typing fast when the hour ends? The break politely waits (capped at +15 minutes)

### 🌍 Polish
- English & German, dark & light theme, everything configurable
- Tray status light: green = running, yellow = update available, red = something broke
- ~30 MB RAM, built with Tauri 2 — no Electron

## ⚠️ Known Limitations

- No overlay over *exclusive* fullscreen games (old DirectX 9 titles) — switch to borderless fullscreen
- Overlay lives on the primary monitor only
- macOS/Linux are best-effort: no flow detection, no lock on macOS, no idle detection on Wayland
- It's the first release — expect rough edges, and please [report them](https://github.com/xDerApfelx/EyeBreak/issues)
